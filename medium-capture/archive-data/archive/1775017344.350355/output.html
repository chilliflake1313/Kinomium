MATHEMATICAL MYSTERIES

You Can Fill It, But You Can’t Paint It

The Strange Case of Gabriel’s Horn

Vagelis Plevris
8 min read
·
1 day ago

There is a peculiar trumpet in mathematics.

It is not made of brass or wood. It is not something you can hold or hear. It exists only as an exact mathematical construction, defined by a simple equation. And yet, despite its simplicity, it behaves in a way that feels almost impossible.

Imagine this horn stretching out into space, longer and longer, becoming thinner and thinner, never quite ending.

Now consider this. If I gave you a gallon of paint, you could pour it inside and fill it completely.

But if I asked you to paint its outer surface, even all the paint in the world would not be enough.

The same object can be filled with a finite amount of paint, and at the same time requires an infinite amount to cover.

That is the paradox of Gabriel’s horn.
